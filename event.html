Satuday club - Kids praising the Lord
February 1st, 2014

The activities initially covered bringing back to school dropout children with a potential whose single parents were too poor to pay for their school fees. Then it extended to looking after street children, providing them with lunch, clothes and school supplies. Access to education seems impossible for them. Myriame holds a Bible school in her small yard for some 80 poor children in her suburb every Saturday except on the holidays after which they are provided a small brunch made up of rice with little fish and milk diluted with tea and Malagasy pancake. But over the years, Myriame addressed providing care and a monthly pension support to elderly people left by themselves and forced to work at an advanced age to earn their living. She provides them with a token pension and some foodstuff every month.

Myriame engaged in helping out urban young people from destitute families but with a potential, go to private universities. Also the majority of the people helped out are single mothers or battered women left stranded with many children to feed and the Lord laid the burden on her heart to help pastors, national and international missionaries, and an orphanage.
